Abep-UK

The Association of Brazilian Postgraduate Students and Researchers in the United Kingdom (Abep-UK) is a not-for-profit civil society based in London that brings together Brazilian researchers and postgraduate students. Abep-UK was founded in February of 1980.

The Abep-UK aims are:

  • To bring together Brazilian reseachers and postgraduate students;
  • To represent its members and to forward their demands;
  • To promote and to foster cultural activities, such as debates with scientific, politicial, social and economical themes connected to Brazil, as well as, any intelectual work and the social consition of its members;
  • To promote other activities of interest of their members.

Translated from the original, in Portuguese.

I was elected twice for the board of the association taking the roles of Chief Financial Officer (2016-2017) and Chief Executive Officer (2017-2018). During this time working with the board, I could engage with other Brazilian students and researchers in the United Kingdom. I could also help to shape some of the Abep-UK initiatives towards its members. A short list of the activities developed during the period is shown below, split in two sections corrresponding to my two roles at the association.

Chief Financial Officer (2016-2017)

I represented Abep-UK and its member in several events promoted by both the board of the association and/or partners. An example is the International Students Day celebration at House of Lords. This event was promoted by the All-Party Group for International Students. Embassy of Brazil invited the board of Abep-UK.

The board performed a survey with Brazilian scholars in the UK concerning the rules applied by the govermental funding bodies. The work was coordinated by Anderson Brito, Chief Executive Officer at that time, and had support from Embassy of Brazil that helped in approaching the scholars. The full report (in Portuguese) can be downloaded here.

Abep-UK released in 2017 a Guide for Brazilian students and Researchers in the United Kingdom. The guide was developed with Embassy of Brazil from a previous text started in the 1980s and updated over time called 'Manual da Ilha' [Guide of the Island]. The new guide was released at the opening ceremony of the IX Conference Abep-UK that took place at Embassy of Brazil with the presence of the Ambassador of Brazil for the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland and Abep-UK honorary president, HE Eduardo dos Santos and delegates of partner associations, e.g., PARSUK, APEC, and SRUK. The guide is free and can be downloaded here.

A considerable part of the efforts of the 2016-2017 board was focused on improving Abep-UK communication. We aimed for a stronger impact on social networks and a better experience in the website, that was reestructured. On Facebook we have achieved an increase of 21% in the number of followers of the page that plummeted from 1793 to 2173 people, featuring an average of 7.5 new followers per week.

We have also organised three editions of the traditional series of talks historically promoted by Abep-UK, called 'Café Brasil'. These talks aim to discuss topics that are relevant to Brazil and or Braziliand students/researchers. The themes of the talks were “Violência sexual e diversidade de gênero: os desafios para a sociedade Brasileira” [Sexual violence and gender diversity: challenges for the Brazilian society], "Hate and Intolerance in Brazil", and "Anti-corruption fight in Brazil: developments and contradictions". They took place in Glasgow, Leeds, and Birmingham, repectively. The Café Brasil that happened in Leeds had the journalist Leandro Sakamoto as speaker.

The IX Abep-UK Conference took place on the 10th of June of 2017 at Imperial College London. The theme of the conference was "Science and Social Development: Challenges In Times of Crisis". For more information, please visit the conference website. Abep-UK had support from Embassy of Brazil and Brazil Forum to organise this conference.

More details about the activities developed during the period can be found on the Final report (in Portuguese).
